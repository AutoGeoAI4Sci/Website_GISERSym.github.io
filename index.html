--- title: "2026 AAG Symposium on GIScience for Human Experience and Responsibility (GISER)" --- # 2026 AAG Symposium on GIScience for Human Experience and Responsibility (GISER) Recent advances in GIScience—including geospatial artificial intelligence (GeoAI), emerging sensing technologies, and large-scale geospatial data—are rapidly transforming how we study **human experience of place** and **human–environment relationships**. Researchers increasingly leverage geotagged texts and images to map emotion and place identity, use street-view imagery to model built environments and perceived safety, and develop new GeoAI methods that support human creativity in map-making and urban design. Collectively, this work highlights a shift toward a more **human-centered, humanistic GIScience** that contextualizes spatial activity with subjective experience, cultural meaning, and social context. At the same time, the ethical implications of these technologies have become impossible to ignore: geoprivacy risks, bias amplification, limited transparency of black-box models, and uncertainty in AI-generated answers. Integrating **responsible thinking** into GIScience is both timely and imperative—shaping how we frame geospatial questions, choose data and algorithms, interpret results, and anticipate misuse. In this symposium, we invite sessions broadly contributing to **human-centered and responsible GIScience**, with emphasis on understanding human–environment interactions, promoting quality of life, and prioritizing human values in geospatial data science. --- ## Call for Participation To join this symposium, please submit your **session proposal(s)** to an organizing committee member **before October 30, 2025**. If you have questions, please contact: - Lei Zou (lzou@tamu.edu) - Yuhao Kang (yuhao.kang@austin.utexas.edu) --- ## Topics of Interest (examples) We welcome topics including, but not limited to: - Advanced GIScience methods (e.g., GeoAI, Spatial Data Science, Digital Twins) for modeling human–environment relationships - Human subjective experiences (emotion, perception, cognition) across places and scales - Human behavior, mobility, and spatiotemporal dynamics - Creativity in cartography, urban design, and geovisualization - Responsible/ethical GIScience, bias mitigation, and geoprivacy - GIScience applications for public health, hazards, housing, crime, transportation, and social good - AI literacy in teaching and learning --- ## Planned Sessions Please see **Program** for the current list of planned sessions.
